Important business logic:

- Sales plans can generate production requirements.
- Production programs use Bill of Materials to calculate raw material needs.
- Material requirements compare required quantity with available raw material stock.
- If there is shortage, the system should generate purchase requirements.
- Purchase requests lead to purchase invoices.
- Purchase invoices lead to goods receipts.
- Goods receipts increase raw material stock.
- Delivery to sales representative moves stock from main warehouse to representative car warehouse.
- Sales invoices reduce stock from representative car warehouse or finished goods warehouse.
- No inventory quantity should be changed directly.
- Every stock change must create a stock movement record.
- Approved transactions only should affect stock.
